Ari Kourkoumelis Job

Ari Kourkoumelis is currently working as a partner at Kourkoumelis & Fotopoulos PLLC. He co-founded the firm in September 2021 and has been working there ever since. Right before that, Ari was working at the United State Department of Commerce as a Senior Counsel, at the Office of the General Counsel from August 2020 to January 2021 and from December 2019 to August 2020 before that.

Ari began his career as a lawyer in 2013 working as an intern at Legal Intern Manhattan District Attorney’s Office. The internship lasted for three months. Then, he was also a student attorney at Post-Conviction Innocence Clinic from August 2013 to December 2013.

In 2014, Supreme Court, Appellate Division Second Judicial Department hired him as a judicial extern. He served there for five months. In the same year, he had also worked at Wraparound Equities Inc. as a summer intern for three months.

Furthermore, in 2015, Ari worked at the United States Court of International Trade as a judicial intern for five months. Then in 2015, he worked at Thor Equities as a legal intern.

After graduating from law school, Ari worked at the U.S. Court of International Trade as a judicial law clerk. In June 2018, he joined McGuireWoods LLP as an associate where he worked till November 2019.

Ari has also volunteered at The Federation of Hellenic Societies of Greater New York as a vice president. He is also a member of the American Hellenic Education Progressive Association till November 2019.

Ari earned his J.D. from New York Law School. He earned his bachelor’s degree in philosophy from Adelphi University in 2013. Furthermore, he attended Columbia Law School where he studied International Business/Trade/Commerce.

Ari Kourkoumelis Family

Ari Kourkoumelis is the son of Thomas “Tom” Kourkoumelis and Kathy Kourkoumelis. Thomas who was born in April 1952 is currently 70 years old. He is currently based in Southold New York. Back in 2014, he was featured in one of the stories of the New York Times in an article titled, A Parthenon of Produce at United Brothers Fruit Market in Astoria.

At the time, Tom was the only United Brothers brother who was not retired to Florida and spent 10 hours at the store most days, as he has for the better part of four decades (not counting the occasional vacation in Greece).

In 1969, Tom and his brothers, Jerry and George, joined the wave of Greeks escaping economic stagnation, island-hopping from Cephalonia to Manhattan, unknowingly alongside two other brothers, Spiro and Damianos Matafias. In New York, the quintet met and sought fruit. But what they found made them long for the old country.

So in 1974, they opened United Brothers Fruit Market in Washington Heights, Manhattan. The crime led to their second move: They landed in Astoria a few years later.

Over the past four decades, Tom Kourkoumelis has managed to achieve the American dream. He says he is “in love” with Astoria, which is why he keeps most of his businesses based in the heart of Hellenism in New York. Mr. Kourkoumelis was but a boy when he set off in search of a better life in the new world. However, despite his success, he never forgot his hometown of Simotata on the Ionian island of Cephalonia.

In fact, he passed on his love for Simotata to his wife Kathy, and his two children, Joanna and Aristidis. Tom Kourkoumelis was born in Simotata to Aristidis and Irene Kourkoumelis – the second of four children.
