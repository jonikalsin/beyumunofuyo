Sonya Alicia Curry, born on May 30, 1966, in Radford, Virginia, is a noteworthy figure known for her exceptional career as an educator and her role as the mother of renowned basketball players, Stephen Curry and Seth Curry.

Early Life and Education

Sonya Alicia Adams, as she was known before marriage, was born to Cleive and Candy Adams. She grew up in challenging circumstances, facing poverty and racist experiences with the Ku Klux Klan.

Despite the adversities, she excelled in sports during high school, participating in volleyball, track and field, and basketball.

Her athletic prowess continued in college, where she attended Virginia Tech as a student athlete, earning accolades in volleyball. Sonya pursued her passion for education and obtained a degree in the field.

Authorship and Podcasting

Sonya Curry's passion for family and faith has been encapsulated in her memoir, "Fierce Love: A Memoir of Family, Faith, and Purpose," released in May 2022.

This literary work delves into her experiences of motherhood, her unwavering faith during challenging times, and her unwavering commitment to education.

She has also ventured into the realm of podcasting as a host on "Raising Fame," a platform that delves into the childhood backstories of iconic athletes.

Community Involvement

Beyond her professional endeavors, Sonya Curry remains deeply engaged in her communities, serving on various boards of directors such as the Charlotte Housing Authority. Her dedication to giving back to the community is evident through her involvement in fundraising and her service at women’s shelters.

Family and Legacy

Sonya Curry's impact extends far beyond her professional life. As the mother of three children and the grandmother of six, she has played a pivotal role in nurturing and guiding her family.

Her memoir, "Fierce Love," stands as a testament to her remarkable journey, offering insights into her experiences and her unwavering faith.
