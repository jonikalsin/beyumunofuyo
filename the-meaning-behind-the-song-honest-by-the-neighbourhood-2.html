The Meaning Behind The Song: “Honest” by The Neighbourhood

Music has a way of touching our hearts and souls, evoking emotions and memories we may have long forgotten. One such song that holds a special place in my heart is “Honest” by The Neighbourhood. Released as a non-album single for the 2014 film The Amazing Spider-Man 2, this track perfectly captures the raw emotions of longing and honesty.

Listening to “Honest” takes me back to a particular moment in my life, a time when I was going through a turbulent relationship. The lyrics, delivered with haunting vocals by lead singer Jesse Rutherford, resonated deeply with me as I grappled with the complexities of honesty within a partnership.

The Struggle With Honesty

The song opens with the lyrics, “Patience, test my patience. If I made it too hard for you, maybe you should’ve changed it.” These lines convey the frustration and self-reflection that arise when we question whether we have contributed to the difficulties in a relationship. It prompts us to consider whether the lack of honesty stems from our own actions.

Continuing into the chorus, The Neighbourhood sings, “I wish you could be honest with me, with me.” This plea for honesty reflects the universal desire for open communication, vulnerability, and transparency in relationships. It speaks to the pain of feeling deceived or misled by someone we deeply care about.

The Weight of Deception

The second verse brings up the haunting question of why someone would choose to stay in a relationship if they were not truly committed. It speaks directly to the feelings of betrayal and confusion experienced when deception is uncovered. The lyrics hold the weight of disappointment and the hope for clarity.

The chorus is repeated multiple times throughout the song, emphasizing the yearning for honesty. It is a plea for the other person to reveal their true self, no matter how difficult it may be. The repetition of this chorus reflects the significance of this plea and the importance placed on genuine communication.

Personal Reflection and Power of Change

As I listened to “Honest” during those difficult times in my life, it became a source of solace and a reminder that change can be incredible. The pre-chorus, “I hope you find a way to be yourself someday, in weakness or in strength,” serves as a powerful message of self-acceptance and growth.

The song encourages both parties to embrace their true selves and recognizes that change, even when painful, can lead to something amazing. It acknowledges the possibility of personal development and the power of authenticity in relationships.

About The Song

“Honest” was produced by Jesse Rutherford, Zach Abels, Jeremy Freedman, and Mikey Margott. It was written by Mikey Margott, Jeremy Freedman, Zach Abels, and Jesse Rutherford. The song was released as part of The Amazing Spider-Man 2 official soundtrack on April 7, 2014.

Conclusion

“Honest” by The Neighbourhood is a powerful and introspective song that explores the struggles of honesty within relationships. It captures the emotions associated with deception, longing for authenticity, and the transformative power of change. This song continues to be a reminder that honesty and communication are essential for meaningful connections.

So, let “Honest” be a reminder to us all to strive for open and honest communication in our relationships, and to never shy away from being true to ourselves.
